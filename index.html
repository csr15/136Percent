About us

We are accomplished psychologists, trusted colleagues, and long-time fun friends. As psychologists, we understand the principles of behavior that keep us apart and those that help us to come together. During this time of racial reckoning, we aim to apply these principles to build social trust among a coalition of Black and White women invested in achieving racial equity.Learn more about us and our work: Dr. Deborah L. Plummer and Dr. Lori Stevic-Rust

Our why

  • In fight for racial equality
  • We believe in the collective power of Black and White women to take us from advocacy to transformative culture change.
  • We believe that the quality of our interactions and how we come together to achieve racial equity is critical to our democracy.
  • We believe that social trust, or the belief in the honesty, fairness and benevolence of each other, is both an emotion and a choice.
  • We believe that this is a moment in history when a critical mass of Black and White women can come together and make a choice to carry everyone forward toward that more perfect union.

Our goal

  • The 2016 and 2020 Presidential elections exposed both the unifying and dividing factors between White and Black women, further deepening our historical divide. We know that trust between Black and White women is a critical factor to lessening that divide.
  • Through a series of shared experiences, the 136% Project aims to enhance social trust as a foundational aspect of an informed citizenry of Black and White women invested in racial equity.

Our 136% Project Experiences

  • The 136% Project gets its name from the 91% Black women and 45% White women voters who support racial equity as a pillar of the Biden/Harris Build Back Better plan. It is comprised of Listening Circles, a Documentary Film, and Social Trust Forums:
  • 136% Project Listening Circles: Enlightened, forward-moving conversations about race between Black and White women will be facilitated by Dr. Debbie and Dr. Lori (2021-2022).
  • 136% Project Documentary Film: With data gathered from the Listening Circles, we’ll produce a documentary describing how Black and White women experience contemporary race relations in America. (2022-2023)
  • 136% Project Social Trust Forums: We’ll form learning communities to strengthen bonds between Black and White women. Together we’ll explore strategies for living within the duality of race and gender and develop competencies for the necessary individual and collective work of racial equity. (2023-2024)
